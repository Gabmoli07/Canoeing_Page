New York

Kennebec River
 
The Kennebec River offers consistent class 2-4 whitewater rafting every day from May through October. It’s an ideal rafting trip for first time rafters, and everyone who enjoys a good mix of whitewater action, fire-grilled lunch on a river beach, and a peaceful afternoon float to the take-out.

 

Penobscot river


 Epic best describes whitewater rafting the West Branch of the Penobscot River, under the shadow of Mount Katahdin. We launch rafting trips in the steep rock walls of Ripogenus Gorge, through the class V rapids of The Cribworks to raft surfing at Big Amberjack. After lunch get set to tackle Nesowadnehunk Falls 11′ foot drop, and the rapids of the lower river.

 

Allegany State Park-Quaker Area

New York is home to some of the greatest waterways on the East Coast. Team up with professional guides at Sacandaga Outdoor Center to enjoy tubing, rafting, and kayaking on the Hudson and Saranac Rivers. Just minutes from Lake George, this family-friendly outfitter is great for groups and for introducing the kids to the outdoors.

 

New Jersey

Delaware Water Gap National Recreation Area

Located in the northwest corner of New Jersey, the park runs along 40 miles of the Delaware River, including where it cuts through a ridge in the Appalachian Mountains to form the famed “gap.” The park is a magnet for kayak enthusiasts.

 

 

 

Lakeview Marina  

Being on New Jersey's largest lake means that there are plenty of places for kayakers to paddle and explore. Lakeview offers Kayak rentals, and has a boat ramp for launching kayaks.

 

Pennsylvania

Youghiogheny River

The Youghiogheny River flows through one of Pennsylvania's largest state parks, Ohiopyle, in the Laurel Highlands region in southwestern Pennsylvania. Locals call it the Yough The river is popular for white water rafting and kayaking.The loop is perfect for kayakers since they do not need a transport vehicle.
